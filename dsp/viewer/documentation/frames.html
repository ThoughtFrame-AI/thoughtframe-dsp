Frames & Interpretation

Frames are the interpretation layer that consumes acoustic windows and events produced by the Python runtime. They exist outside the signal processing system and operate exclusively on persisted evidence, not raw audio streams.

Separation of Concerns

The acoustic runtime is intentionally limited to evidence production. It does not assign meaning, intent, or consequence to detected activity. Frames exist to handle those responsibilities separately.

This separation enforces a hard boundary:

  • The Python system measures and segments
  • Frames interpret and reason
  • Humans remain authoritative over conclusions

By design, interpretation can evolve without invalidating previously captured evidence.

What a Frame Represents

In this context, a Frame represents a stateful workspace focused on one or more acoustic events. A Frame may span:

  • A single window
  • A sequence of related windows
  • A hypothesis that evolves over time

Frames accumulate context as new evidence arrives, allowing interpretations to be revised rather than replaced.

Evidence Consumption

Frames ingest structured window records rather than live streams. Each record includes precise timing, aggregate statistics, and optional audio references.

Because evidence is immutable once written, Frames can:

  • Replay historical events
  • Compare interpretations across runs
  • Apply new reasoning logic to old data

This decoupling is essential for auditability and long-term analysis.

Human-in-the-Loop by Design

Frames are designed to support human review, not replace it. Analysts interact with Frames by:

  • Reviewing window statistics and timelines
  • Listening to associated audio segments
  • Annotating, tagging, or rejecting interpretations
  • Recording confidence or uncertainty

Human conclusions are layered on top of evidence rather than overwriting it. Disagreement and revision are first-class outcomes.

State and Temporal Reasoning

Frames maintain state across time. Rather than producing isolated classifications, they track how interpretations change as additional evidence arrives.

This enables reasoning patterns such as:

  • Escalation from weak signals to strong hypotheses
  • Decay or dismissal of unsupported interpretations
  • Correlation across multiple events or sensors

State is explicit and inspectable, avoiding hidden model memory.

Multiple Interpretations

Because Frames do not modify underlying evidence, multiple interpretations may coexist. Different Frames may analyze the same windows using different assumptions, policies, or levels of confidence.

This allows the system to support:

  • Parallel analytic workflows
  • Training and evaluation scenarios
  • Post-hoc analysis with updated criteria

Boundary to Action

Frames may inform downstream actions — alerts, reports, or integrations — but they do not directly control the acoustic runtime.

Any action taken is traceable back to:

  • The original signal
  • The windowed evidence
  • The Frame state at the time of decision
  • Human input, if present

Why This Matters

By separating evidence production from interpretation, the system remains adaptable without becoming opaque. Frames allow reasoning to evolve while preserving the integrity of the underlying signal record.

This design is critical in real-time sensing environments where trust, accountability, and hindsight matter as much as responsiveness.
